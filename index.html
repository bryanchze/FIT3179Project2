HOW DOES THESE FACTORS AFFECT HAPPINESS
IN DIFFERENT COUNTRIES?



These factors attempt to explain the self-reported, or perceived happiness of each country. These factors aim to explain how the economy (GDP per Capita), family, freedom, generosity, health, and trust in the government influence our happiness. The percentage of these factors that affects the happiness score can vary significantly from country to country, but generally, the most significant factors incluencing our happiness are our family, our health as well as the state of the nation's economy.

HOW DOES HAPPINESS AND IT'S FACTORS VARY
IN DIFFERENT REGIONS OF THE WORLD?

The world average happiness score stayed close over the past 5 years with very slight fluctuations in 2017 and 2019. We can see that the average world happiness level did not drastically change over 2015-2019. Here, we observe the difference on the happiness score as well as the factors that affects happiness over time between different regions.

WHERE ARE THE 10 HAPPIEST PEOPLE
IN THE WORLD?


We can see that most of the countries in the top 10 are located in Europe. There are three regions that are in the top 10 happiest countries in the world which are Europe, North America and Ocenia. At the country level, we can see that there are some fluctuations in the rankings from year to year. However, if we look at the rankings by region, we can observe that every year the regions in which the happiest countries are located has not really change much which solidifies the evidence to the happiness in these particular regions. If we were to compare to the least happiness countries, we will see that they would have almost twice the number of countries during the same period, mostly located in Africa.

WHAT ARE THE CHANGES IN HAPPINESS
OVER TIME?

Although the world happiness average did no change much, we can observe that there's a significant difference between the countries when it come to the happiness score. In 2019, the difference between the highest and lowest score was of 4.9 points. And we can see that this difference is actually increasing through the years.

IS THERE A CORRELATION BETWEEN THESE FACTORS
AND THE HAPPINESS SCORE?

We present scatterplots of five main factors against the happiness score to see if there is any correlation between these factors with the happiness score. The factors that most influences happiness are economy, family support, health and freedom to make life choices, whereas the trust in the government does not show much of an incluence to happiness. There is no doubt that the factors measured in the World Happiness Report contributes significantly to our individual happiness.

However, we should also take into consideration on other factors that could possibly be at play. For example, the sustainable development goals that was designed to improve the live of people on Earth such as no poverty, quality education, affordable and clean energy and many more. Therefore, these factors might also need to be looked into in regions that are unhappy in order to understand the reason behind it.
