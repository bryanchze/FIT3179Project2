Happiness is when one is in a state of well-being and contentment in what they are doing or in life in general. If that is the case, then it comes to no suprise that happiness varies in different countries in the world. HOW DOES THESE FACTORS AFFECT HAPPINESS
IN DIFFERENT COUNTRIES?



These factors attempt to explain the self-reported, or perceived happiness of each country. These factors aim to explain how the economy (GDP per Capita), family, freedom, generosity, health, and trust in the government influence our happiness. The percentage of these factors that affects the happiness score can vary significantly from country to country, but generally, the most significant factors incluencing our happiness are our family, our health as well as the state of the nation's economy.

HOW DOES HAPPINESS AND IT'S FACTORS VARY
IN DIFFERENT REGIONS OF THE WORLD?

The world average happiness score stayed close over the past 5 years with very slight fluctuations in 2017 and 2019 and cannot be considered significant because it is very small. Therefore, we can conclude that the average world happiness level did not drastically change over 2015-2019.

Here, we observe the difference on the happiness score as well as the factors that affects happiness over time between different regions. On average, Australia and New Zealand has the highest average happiness score every year, hence explaining Oceania having the highest average scores for all of the factors that affects happiness for each year.

WHERE ARE THE 10 HAPPIEST PEOPLE
IN THE WORLD?


We can see that most of the countries in the top 10 are located in Europe. There are three regions that are in the top 10 happiest countries in the world which are Europe, North America and Ocenia. At the country level, we can see that there are some fluctuations in the rankings from year to year. However, if we look at the rankings by region, we can observe that every year the regions in which the happiest countries are located has not really change much which solidifies the evidence to the happiness in these particular regions. Moreover, we can also see that Netherlands had also been on the list, gradually moving from 7th place in 2015 to the 5th place in 2019.

WHAT ARE THE CHANGES IN HAPPINESS
OVER TIME?

Similar to the previous section, but we will now go deeper to see the difference between the happiness scores between coutnries over time. Although the world happiness average did no change much, we can observe that there's a significant difference between the countries when it come to the happiness score.

In 2019, the difference between the highest and lowest score was of 4.9 points. And we can see that this difference is actually increasing through the years.

IS THERE A CORRELATION BETWEEN THESE FACTORS
AND THE HAPPINESS SCORE?

Lastly, in order to help us understand what might influence the behaviour of happiness scores, we present scatterplots of five main factors against the happiness score to see if there is any correlation between these factors with the happiness score. We can see that the factors that most influences happiness are economy, family support, health as well as freedom to make life choices, whereas the trust in the government does not show much of an incluence to happiness. So, there is no doubt that the factors measured in the World Happiness

Report contributes significantly to our individual happiness. However, we should also take into consideration on other factors that could possibly be at play. For example, the sustainable development goals that was designed to improve the live of people on Earth such as no poverty, quality education, affordable and clean energy and many more. Therefore, these factors might also need to be looked into in regions that are unhappy in order to understand the reason behind it.
